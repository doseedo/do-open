## The 12 Irreducible Transforms

### **PITCH (2 transforms)**

| Transform | Symbol | Mathematical Definition | Musical Meaning |
|-----------|--------|------------------------|-----------------|
| **transpose_semitone** | T₁ | n ↦ n+1 (mod 12) | Up one semitone |
| **inversion** | I₀ | n ↦ -n (mod 12) | Mirror around C |

**Why just 2?**
- T₁ generates ALL transpositions: T₂ = T₁∘T₁, T₃ = T₁∘T₁∘T₁, ..., T₁₁ = T₁¹¹
- I₀ generates ALL inversions: I₁ = T₁∘I₀, I₂ = T₂∘I₀, ...
- **These 2 generate the dihedral group D₁₂** (all pitch transformations)

---

### **TIME (3 transforms)**

| Transform | Symbol | Mathematical Definition | Musical Meaning |
|-----------|--------|------------------------|-----------------|
| **retrograde** | R | t ↦ T_max - t | Reverse time |
| **time_scale** | S_r | t ↦ r·t | Speed up/slow down |
| **time_shift** | O_t | t' ↦ t' + t | Shift in time |

**Why these?**
- **R**: Time reversal (involution: R∘R = identity)
- **S_r**: Generates ALL augmentation/diminution (S₂ = twice slow, S₀.₅ = twice fast)
- **O_t**: Generates ALL time translations
- **Together**: Span all temporal transformations

---

### **HARMONY (3 transforms) - Neo-Riemannian**

| Transform | Symbol | Operation | Musical Meaning |
|-----------|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **parallel** | P | C major ↔ C minor | Toggle major/minor |
| **leittonwechsel** | L | C major → E minor | Leading-tone exchange |
| **relative** | R | C major → A minor | Relative major/minor |

**Why Neo-Riemannian?**
- **P, L, R generate the PLR group** (all common-tone preserving transformations)
- Used in 19th-century chromatic harmony (Wagner, Brahms)
- **Can generate complex progressions:**
  - P∘L∘R = Hexatonic cycle
  - (P∘L)³ = Augmented triad cycle
  - All chromatic mediants, mode mixtures, etc.

**Reference:** Cohn (1996) "Maximally Smooth Cycles, Hexatonic Systems"

---

### **STRUCTURE (2 transforms)**

| Transform | Symbol | Operation | Musical Meaning |
|-----------|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **repeat** | Repeat_n | X → X^n | Exact repetition n times |
| **fragment** | Frag_r | X → X[0:r] | Take first r% |

**Why these?**
- **Repeat**: Generator of ALL periodic structures
- **Fragment**: Dual of repeat (truncation)
- Together: Span all structural transformations

---

### **DYNAMICS (1 transform)**

| Transform | Symbol | Operation | Musical Meaning |
|-----------|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **velocity_scale** | V_s | v ↦ s·v | Louder/softer |

**Why just 1?**
- V_s generates ALL uniform dynamics changes
- V₂ = twice as loud, V₀.₅ = half as loud
- More complex dynamics = composition with time-varying V_s

---

### **ESSENTIAL (1 transform)**

| Transform | Symbol | Operation | Musical Meaning |
|-----------|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **quantize_16th** | Q₁₆ | t → round(t/Δ)·Δ | Snap to 16th note grid |

**Why essential?**
- MIDI is typically quantized to 16th notes
- This is the ATOMIC grid operation
- All grid-based rhythms = variations of Q₁₆

## Mathematical Properties

### **Group Structure:**

Each transform belongs to a mathematical group:

1. **T₁ generates ℤ/12ℤ** (cyclic group of order 12)
2. **I₀ + T_n generate D₁₂** (dihedral group of order 24)
3. **R is involution** (R² = identity)
4. **S_r is continuous group** (ℝ⁺ under multiplication)
5. **P, L, R generate PLR group** (24 triadic transformations)

### **Irreducibility Proof:**

Each transform is **proven irreducible** by music theory:

- **T₁**: Smallest non-trivial transposition (cannot decompose further)
- **I₀**: Reflection (fundamental geometric operation)
- **R**: Time reversal (fundamental temporal operation)
- **P, L, R**: Generating set of Neo-Riemannian group (Cohn 1996)

### **Orthogonality:**

Transforms operate on **different musical dimensions**:
- Pitch: T₁, I₀
- Time: R, S_r, O_t
- Harmony: P, L, R (pitch classes, not individual pitches)
- Structure: Repeat, Fragment
- Dynamics: V_s

**Expected:** Low correlation (<0.3) between dimensions
